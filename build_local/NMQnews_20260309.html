DID YOU KNOW?
Energy costs – how does our embedded network stack up?
Comparing Altogether Group rates
We’ve conducted a comparison of our current electricity arrangement with Altogether Group against the next cheapest retail plans available on the Australian Government's comparison site Energy Made Easy. The short answer: the embedded network is delivering genuine savings for every owner in the scheme.
This article gives an overview of how our electricity supply works and even includes a link to an interactive tool so you can see the results of our cost comparison yourself.
How our electricity supply works
Northmarque operates an embedded electricity network managed by Altogether Group. Rather than each lot having a separate connection to the Energex grid, the body corporate purchases electricity in bulk through a single ‘gate meter’ at a wholesale rate. Altogether then meters and bills each lot individually based on actual usage.
This arrangement gives the scheme collective purchasing power that an individual household would not have. It also means our supply and usage rates are different from the standard retail plans you’d see advertised by energy companies.

How does that compare to other options?
We reviewed the next 9 cheapest single-rate, fixed-price electricity plans available for our postcode (Carseldine, 4034) on Energy Made Easy. Every single retail plan was more expensive than Altogether Group.
For a 2–person household, Altogether Groups is at least $260 cheaper and up to an incredible $700 cheaper per year than any of the providers we compared.
It’s better than just the savings
There is an important factor that these numbers don't show. The retail plan rates are bundled rates – they include both energy charges and network distribution charges, because those plans are designed for customers connected directly to the Energex grid.
You do have the Power of Choice though. If you want to switch, you can! But you should know…
If you were to switch to an external retailer, you would not pay the bundled rate. Instead, you need to ask the retailer for an ‘energy only’ offer (just the electricity itself). Then you would continue paying network charges to Altogether Group. That means a higher total cost than what the retail comparison websites show. Oh, and 2 bills!
In short: the retail plans are already more expensive on paper – and the real cost of switching from inside the embedded network would be higher still.
A few other things to consider
As we've said, you do have the legal right to choose an external retailer, but there are practical costs and complications on top of getting 2 bills.
1. Metering
A new retailer may need to replace the Altogether meter with their own. This may come with a connection fee and a brief period without supply during the changeover.
2. Retailer willingness
Not all retailers are willing to make offers to embedded network customers. The administrative complexity and low volume make it unattractive for some providers.
3. You pay all costs
All opt-out costs sit with the individual owner. Exit fees are capped at $20 in Queensland, but metering and connection charges are always additional and can vary.
